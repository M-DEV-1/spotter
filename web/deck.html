The problem

Robot policies fail silently.

A Vision-Language-Action model closes its gripper on empty air, nudges the object out of reach, or stalls — and just keeps going. It has no one watching it who can say "that didn't work — try again, but reach 5cm further forward."

Gripper closed, empty

The classic miss. Hand shuts on nothing and the policy never notices.

Object knocked away

A clumsy approach shoves the cube out of the target region.

No progress

The arm drifts, stalls, and the episode times out with nothing achieved.

SPOTTER02 — the problem
The architecture

A frozen actor + an external coach.

We never touch the policy's weights. Gemma supervises from the outside — exactly like a human spotter calling corrections to an athlete.

Frozen VLA
Pi0 (LIBERO / Franka) drives the arm from pixels + state. Weights never change.
→
Cheap signals
Sim ground-truth: gripper-empty, object-out-of-region, no-progress.
→
Gemma 4 31B
Reads frame + signals, rewrites the instruction in language.
→
Re-conditioned actor
VLA re-samples its trajectory on Gemma's new words. The loop closes.

The causal claim: Gemma's words change what the robot does. Not waypoints — a rewritten task.

SPOTTER03 — architecture
Failure detection

Cheap signals trigger. Gemma interprets.

We don't ask the model to guess failure from raw pixels. The simulator hands it free ground-truth flags; Gemma does the semantic work of explaining and fixing.

What the sim detects (free)

gripper_closed_empty
cube_out_of_region
pose_mismatch
no_progress_timeout

What Gemma adds (semantic)

Why it failed · which direction the cube actually is · how to rewrite the instruction so the next attempt works — bounded to a safe workspace.

SPOTTER04 — failure signals
Live supervisor trace · real run @ step 1000

Perception → Planner → Validator → fix.

PERCEPTION
1.85s"The gripper is closed and hovering above the table, but it is empty. A green cube sits on the table, in front of the gripper."
PLANNER
2.35s"Open the gripper, move forward to center over the cube, and re-grasp — it closed without grasping because it was positioned behind the cube."
VALIDATOR
2.93sREJECTED: too vague — no distance. Must stay inside workspace bounds x∈[0.3,1.0], y∈[-0.5,0.5], z∈[0,0.15].
FINAL
"Open the gripper, move the arm forward by 5cm to center over the green cube, descend to grasp height, and re-grasp."
SPOTTER05 — the supervisor self-corrects before it ever touches the robot
Why Cerebras

Fast inference buys continuous supervision.

A slow supervisor can only check a few keyframes before the scene drifts. A fast one watches at ~1 Hz and catches the failure the moment it happens — while the cube is still reachable.

~2–3s
full 3-agent correction, end to end
1 Hz
supervision loop (well under 100 RPM)
0
policy weights changed

Honest + narrow: only token generation speeds up — network round-trip is unchanged. The point is sooner intervention, not a different result.

SPOTTER07 — the descope ladder
What this is — and isn't

An exploration, openly built on prior work.

  • We reproduced and explored an FPC-VLA-style semantic supervision pattern — we did not invent it.
  • The contribution is engineering: a fast, multi-agent, self-validating coach wired to a frozen VLA in sim.
  • The supervisor outputs a rewritten instruction, not raw waypoints — a coach, not a planner.

Prior art: FPC-VLA (2509.04018) · RoboFAC (2505.12224) · CycleVLA (2601.02295)
Lineage: SayCan (2022) · Inner Monologue (2022) · Code as Policies (2023)
